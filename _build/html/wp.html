wp.wp(ns, pos)[source]¶

Based on https://www.cs.williams.edu/~freund/cs326/ReasoningPart1.html

We usually want to use the precondition that guarantees correctness for the broadest set of inputs. Stated differently, we want the weakest precondition: the most general precondition needed to establish the postcondition. The terms “weak” and “strong” refer to how general or specific an assertion is. The weaker an assertion is, the more general it is; the stronger it is, the more specific it is. We write P = wp(S,Q) to indicate that P is the weakest precondition for statement S and postcondition Q.
